فَضَائِلُ لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ
What This Night Carries
From the Quran and authenticated Sunnah
BENEFIT I
🌙
لَيْلَةُ ٱلْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ
Surah Al-Qadr 97:3
"The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months."
One thousand months is 83 years and 4 months — more than a full human lifetime. A single night of worship surpasses a lifetime of deeds.
BENEFIT II
📖
إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَـٰهُ فِى لَيْلَةٍ مُّبَـٰرَكَةٍ
Surah Ad-Dukhan 44:3
"Indeed, We sent it down on a blessed night."
The Quran — every word of which is mercy and guidance for all of humanity — was first revealed to the Prophet ﷺ on this very night. The night is blessed because the Book is blessed.
BENEFIT III
✨
تَنَزَّلُ ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةُ وَٱلرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم
Surah Al-Qadr 97:4
"The angels and the Spirit descend therein by permission of their Lord."
Jibreel (عليه السلام) himself descends. The scholars explain that the angels descend in numbers greater than the pebbles of the earth — and their passage through the atmosphere is what subdues the sun's rays the following morning.
BENEFIT IV
🤲
مَنْ قَامَ لَيْلَةَ الْقَدْرِ إِيمَانًا وَاحْتِسَابًا غُفِرَ لَهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِنْ ذَنْبِهِ
Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim — Mutawatir
"Whoever stands in prayer on Laylat al-Qadr out of faith and seeking reward, his previous sins are forgiven."
This is among the most authenticated narrations in all of Islam — narrated by multiple companions through multiple chains. Complete forgiveness of past sins in a single night.
BENEFIT V
☮️
سَلَـٰمٌ هِىَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ ٱلْفَجْرِ
Surah Al-Qadr 97:5
"It is peace until the emergence of dawn."
Ibn Kathir explains that the angels greet every believer in worship throughout the entire night. The night is filled with salaam — peace descends on every soul that stands, prays, and remembers.
BENEFIT VI
💫
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
Sahih Ibn Majah — Aisha (رضي الله عنها)
"O Allah, You are Pardoning, You love to pardon, so pardon me."
Aisha asked the Prophet ﷺ what to say if she knew it was Laylat al-Qadr. This dua was his answer. The best dua for this night is not a request for wealth or health — it is a request for pardon. That is the wisdom of the Prophet ﷺ.
BENEFIT VII
📋
فِيهَا يُفْرَقُ كُلُّ أَمْرٍ حَكِيمٍ
Surah Ad-Dukhan 44:4
"Every precise matter is decided on that night."
Ibn Abbas explained that provisions, lifespans, and all affairs of the coming year are decreed on this night and handed to the angels for execution. What you do on this night may shape what is written for your entire year.

الأَسَاسُ الشَّرْعِيّ
The Basis
Why we observe the sky — and why the date is hidden
Allah's Command to Look at the Sky

Allah commands the believer to look at the sky with reflection (tadabbur). This app is an act of worship — purposeful observation of what Allah has placed in the heavens as signs.

SURAH QAF 50:6
أَفَلَمْ يَنظُرُوا إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ فَوْقَهُمْ كَيْفَ بَنَيْنَـٰهَا وَزَيَّنَّـٰهَا وَمَا لَهَا مِن فُرُوجٍ
"Have they not looked at the sky above them — how We structured it and adorned it, and it has no rifts?"
SURAH AL-MULK 67:3–4
فَٱرْجِعِ ٱلْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَىٰ مِن فُطُورٍ ثُمَّ ٱرْجِعِ ٱلْبَصَرَ كَرَّتَيْنِ يَنقَلِبْ إِلَيْكَ ٱلْبَصَرُ خَاسِئًا وَهُوَ حَسِيرٌ
"Return your vision to the sky — do you see any breaks? Then look again and again. Your vision will return to you humbled and exhausted."
Why the Date is Hidden
خَرَجْتُ لِأُخْبِرَكُمْ بِلَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ فَتَلَاحَى فُلَانٌ وَفُلَانٌ فَرُفِعَتْ
"I came out to tell you the night of Laylat al-Qadr, but two men were arguing and it was lifted from me. Perhaps that is better for you — so seek it in the 7th, 9th, and 5th from the end."
Sahih Bukhari — narrated by 'Ubadah ibn as-Samit
"Allah concealed Laylat al-Qadr from this ummah as a mercy — so that they would give life to all ten nights in seeking it, not just one."
Ibn Khuzaymah — authenticated
The Prophet's Sign — The Sunrise
Primary Sign — Sahih Muslim
تَطْلُعُ الشَّمْسُ يَوْمَئِذٍ لَا شُعَاعَ لَهَا كَأَنَّهَا طَسْتٌ
"The sun rises that morning with no rays, white, as if it were a plate (tast)." — Ubayy ibn Ka'b (رضي الله عنه)
Additional Detail — Sunan Abu Dawud
"The night is calm, neither hot nor cold. The sun rises in the morning without its piercing radiance — pale, like the full moon on the night of badr (14th night)."
Scholarly Explanation — Musnad Ahmad
"The angels on that night are more numerous than the pebbles of the earth — and their presence is what subdues the sun's rays the following morning." This is why the sign appears the day after, not during the night itself.
Important Note
This is an observational sign given after the night has passed — not a way to predict it, but a way to reflect on whether it has already occurred. The sign is a gift of hope, not a certificate of certainty.

Camera Guide
How to photograph the sign correctly
Why 5 Photos
The sun changes within seconds at sunrise. The hadith sign — if present — is most visible in the first 2 minutes. Five photos ensures you capture the clearest possible frame for scoring.
The 5-Photo Method
1
The moment of Shuruq
Take your first photo the instant the sun's disc clears the horizon. This is the most critical shot — it captures the sun at its weakest and most diagnostic.
AT EXACT SUNRISE TIME
2
One minute after
The sun is now a full disc above the horizon. Capture sun color, ray presence, and disc definition. This is often the clearest shot for scoring.
+1 MINUTE
3
Two minutes after
The sun is strengthening. If the hadith sign is present, rays will still be absent. If they appear now, take note — this is diagnostic information.
+2 MINUTES
4
Three minutes after
By now the sign should be fully visible or clearly absent. A sun still rayless and white at this point is a strong indicator.
+3 MINUTES
5
Wide sky shot
Step back. Photograph the full sky and horizon. This captures atmosphere quality — the calm, clear sky that the hadith describes as part of the night's character.
+3–4 MINUTES
Best Practices
Do: Face east directly. Same spot each day. No zoom (zoom distorts color). No filters. Photograph before the sun rises too high — the window closes fast.

Don't: Use night mode or HDR. Apply any filter or enhancement. Wait until the sun is bright — you'll miss the window.

If cloudy: Heavy cloud cover gives no useful data. Light haze is actually relevant — the hadith describes a clear calm atmosphere. Rate your atmosphere score low for heavy cloud, and note the uncertainty.
